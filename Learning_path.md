
## Goal

Learn to think like:
- Global macro hedge funds
- Rates traders
- FX desks
- Institutional allocators

NOT like:
- News headline reactors
- Retail “event = asset up/down” traders

Core mindset:

> Macro is not:
> “War happened → buy oil”
>
> Macro is:
> “What variable dominates, what is already priced, and where is consensus wrong?”

---

# Phase 1 — Build the Mental Framework (1–2 months)

## 1. Interest Rates (MOST IMPORTANT)

Learn:
- Central bank rates
- Bond yields
- Yield curve
- Real yields
- Rate cuts vs hikes

Understand:
- Most assets are repriced through interest rates
- Low rates pump growth/tech
- High rates compress valuations

Key concepts:
- Duration
- Discount rates
- Bond pricing
- Real yield vs nominal yield

Track daily:
- US 10Y Treasury Yield
- 2Y Yield
- Fed Funds expectations

---

## 2. Inflation

Learn:
- CPI
- PPI
- Sticky inflation
- Wage inflation
- Energy inflation

Understand:
- Inflation changes monetary policy expectations
- Markets react to expectations, not raw numbers

Track:
- CPI releases
- Oil prices
- Shelter inflation
- Wage growth

---

## 3. Liquidity

Learn:
- QE (Quantitative Easing)
- QT (Quantitative Tightening)
- Money supply
- Credit expansion
- Bank lending

Understand:
- Markets are liquidity machines
- Liquidity expansion often inflates asset prices

Key idea:
> Don’t ask “is economy good?”
> Ask “is liquidity expanding or contracting?”

---

## 4. Currency Markets (VERY IMPORTANT)

Learn:
- DXY
- USD strength
- Carry trades
- Currency differentials

Understand:
- FX is the nervous system of macro
- Strong USD impacts global liquidity

Track:
- DXY
- USD/JPY
- EUR/USD

---

# Phase 2 — Learn Cross-Asset Relationships (2–3 months)

Track daily:

| Asset | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| US10Y Yield | Rate expectations |
| DXY | Global dollar stress |
| Oil | Inflation + geopolitics |
| Gold | Real yields / fear |
| S&P500 | Risk appetite |
| VIX | Volatility/fear |
| BTC | Liquidity/speculation |

Daily question:

> Which variable is dominating today?

Examples:
- Rising yields crushing tech
- Oil driving inflation fears
- USD strength pressuring global equities

Goal:
Build pattern recognition.

---

# Phase 3 — Learn Narrative vs Pricing

Retail mindset:
> “War = oil up”

Macro mindset:
> “Was war already priced?”

Learn:
- Expectations
- Positioning
- Consensus
- Reflexivity

Key understanding:
Markets move based on:
- Surprise
- Repricing
- Probability shifts

NOT raw events alone.

---

# Phase 4 — Risk Management & Position Sizing

This is what keeps macro traders alive.

Learn:
- Drawdown management
- Position sizing
- Convexity
- Asymmetric bets
- Risk/reward
- Kelly Criterion (lightly)

Core lesson:
> Survival matters more than being right.

Most macro traders:
- Are wrong often
- Cut losses quickly
- Preserve capital
- Press advantages selectively

---

# Phase 5 — Build a Daily Macro Routine

Every morning track:
- US10Y
- DXY
- Oil
- Gold
- S&P Futures
- Fed expectations

Journal daily:

1. What moved?
2. Why?
3. What variable dominated?
4. What changed in expectations?

Example:
- “Gold down despite war because real yields rose.”

Do this consistently for 6+ months.

Goal:
Develop intuition.

# Phase 7 — Separate Learning From Investing

VERY IMPORTANT.

You do NOT need to:
- Trade every thesis
- Rotate your portfolio constantly
- Bet real money on every idea

Better approach:
- Keep core portfolio simple (VWRA-heavy)
- Paper trade ideas
- Journal predictions
- Review outcomes

Goal:
Learn macro WITHOUT blowing up capital.

# Core Principles

## 1. Markets price expectations
NOT events.

---

## 2. The dominant variable matters most
Sometimes:
- Rates dominate war
- USD dominates inflation
- Liquidity dominates fundamentals

---

## 3. Narratives are seductive
A coherent story does NOT guarantee profit.

---

## 4. Simplicity often wins
Complexity increases:
- emotional attachment
- overtrading
- allocation drift

---

## 5. Risk management > prediction
The best macro traders survive long enough for asymmetry to work.

---

# Your Strengths

You naturally:
- Think systemically
- Connect geopolitics to markets
- Seek causal chains
- Reflect deeply

These are macro-oriented traits.

---

# Your Main Risk

You become emotionally attached to narratives.

Danger pattern:
Narrative → conviction → concentration → drift

Goal:
Become probabilistic, flexible, and detached.

---

# Final Truth

Most macro professionals eventually realize:

> Predicting is hard.
> Risk management matters more.

The goal is not to always be right.

The goal is:
- survive
- adapt
- compound knowledge and capital over time.
